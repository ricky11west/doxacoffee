Executive Summary
March 2026 — Monthly Close
Prepared by TurnkeyCFO  |  April 1, 2026
Doxa Coffee Roasters delivered its strongest month on record in March 2026, with total revenue of $84,320 — up 14.2% from February and 22.1% ahead of the prior year. All three locations contributed positively, led by the Main Street truck which came within 5% of its $40K monthly goal. Gross margin held firm at 61.0%, reflecting disciplined cost management despite rising input costs. Net income of $18,750 is the highest monthly profit in company history, and the business has now crossed the $1M annualized run rate for the first time — a significant milestone. Average ticket size climbed to $12.40, driven by strong food attach rates and retail whole bean bag momentum. The Riverside truck remains the primary growth opportunity, tracking at 78% of its monthly goal with clear upside heading into the spring event season.

Highlights

What’s working this month
Record revenue month. $84,320 is a new all-time high, crossing the $1M annualized run rate for the first time in company history — a landmark milestone.
Margin expansion. Gross margin improved to 61.0% (+0.4pts MoM) despite higher oat milk costs, driven by January menu price adjustments now flowing through fully.
Retail momentum. Whole bean bag sales grew 31% MoM to $5,250, validating the retail shelf strategy and unlocking a high-margin channel with minimal added labor.
Food attach rate up. Avocado toast and breakfast sandwich combined for $12,240 — up 18% vs. February — significantly lifting average per-visit spend.
▼

Risks & Watchpoints

Areas requiring attention
Riverside underperformance. Tracking at only 78% of its $36K goal. Foot traffic data suggests inconsistent operating hours — scheduling needs a full review for April.
Labor cost creeping up. Labor as % of revenue edged to 14.9% at Main St vs. a 13.5% target, driven by one additional part-time hire in March.
Input cost pressure. Coffee bean costs up ~8% YoY from Ethiopia and Colombia suppliers. Current supply contracts expire in June — renegotiation window is now open.
Truck maintenance reserve. Main St truck at 42,000 miles with no major service since Q3. A $3–5K preventative maintenance cost should be budgeted for Q2.
▶

Action Items

Recommended next steps
Review Riverside scheduling — audit operating hours vs. peak demand windows for April. Target minimum 6 hrs/day, 6 days/week to close the revenue gap.
Initiate supplier negotiations on coffee bean contracts before June expiry. Explore locking in a 6-month forward rate to protect gross margin.
Schedule truck service for Main St vehicle in April during a low-traffic weekday. Reserve $4,000 from March net income for this purpose now.
Expand retail shelf at Downtown Café — add 2 new SKUs (single-origin pour-over kits, branded tumblers) in time for Mother’s Day weekend in May.
